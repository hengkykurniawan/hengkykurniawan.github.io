Hi, I'm **buakia** — an economist.

I earned my training in economics at the **University of Indonesia (UI)** and at
the **Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (VU)**, where I built my foundations in
economic theory, applied econometrics, and quantitative policy analysis. Working
across these two academic traditions — one rooted in the realities of a large
emerging economy, the other in the rigorous European research environment — has
shaped how I approach economic questions: empirically grounded, but always
connected to the bigger picture.

### What I work on

My interests sit at the intersection of **applied microeconomics**,
**econometrics**, and **public policy**. I enjoy using data to understand how
people, firms, and institutions actually behave, and translating that evidence
into ideas that can inform real decisions. Panel data methods, causal
inference, and economic modelling are tools I keep coming back to.

### This site

I use this space to share teaching material, handouts, and short notes on topics
I find interesting — from econometric techniques to current economic issues. Feel
free to browse the [blog](/blog) and the [material](/material) pages.
